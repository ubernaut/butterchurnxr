Butterchurn XR

MilkDrop music visuals, projected onto your real room in mixed reality.

What is this? Butterchurn is a WebGL port of the legendary MilkDrop visualizer from Winamp. This fork adds WebXR: on a mixed-reality headset (like a Meta Quest 3) it paints the visuals across the actual walls, floor, ceiling, and furniture of the room you're standing in.

Audio: the page asks for your microphone right away — allow it and the visuals react to whatever is playing around you. Prefer files? Use the buttons below instead. Space bar or arrow keys switch presets, and the panel below previews the visualizer.

On a headset: run Space Setup first so the browser knows your room, then press Enter AR (the button appears when your device supports it). Up to 5 simultaneous visualizations are dealt across your surfaces; press A or B on the right controller for new random presets, and choose whether real furniture occludes the visuals, gets painted too, or is cut out live by the depth sensor.
